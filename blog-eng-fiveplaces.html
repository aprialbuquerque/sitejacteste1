If you're in Rio de Janeiro and looking to dance, you're in for a treat! Rio is home to some of the best forró and samba de gafieira venues in Brazil.



Best Places to Dance FORRÓ in the SOUTH ZONE of Rio de Janeiro: Three Unmissable Options


1. Vuvu Tuesdays - Botafogo, Rio de Janeiro: Located in Botafogo, the Forró nights at Vuvu Restaurant offer a lively and relaxed atmosphere, perfect for dancing Forró into the early hours. With live music and lots of fun, this is a must-visit experience for Forró lovers in the South Zone of Rio de Janeiro. Additionally, the venue offers mouth-watering cuisine accompanied by incredible and creative drinks.
Address: Rua Sorocaba, 601, Botafogo, Rio de Janeiro/RJ.


2. Praça São Salvador - Laranjeiras, Rio de Janeiro On Fridays, Praça São Salvador turns into a true Forró haven. With live music and contagious energy, it's the perfect place to dance under the stars and enjoy the relaxed and lively atmosphere of Rio de Janeiro.
Address: Praça São Salvador, Laranjeiras, Rio de Janeiro/RJ.


3. Dominguinhos de Pife at TLT Bar - Botafogo, Rio de Janeiro For a more intimate experience, Dominguinhos de Pife at TLT Bar is the ideal choice. With a cozy atmosphere and quality music, this venue is perfect for a special night to the sound of the best Forró.
Address: Rua Visconde Silva, 14, Botafogo, Rio de Janeiro/RJ.


SURPRISE OPTION FROM THE LIST: JATô no Forró - Tijuca, Rio de Janeiro Although not in the South Zone, JATô no Forró is an unmissable option that we cannot fail to mention for dance lovers. With a Forró trio, air conditioning and a pleasant environment, this event, which happens approximately every two months, is an excellent option for those seeking a night of fun and dance. If you're looking for a cooler option, with seating and cheap drinks, for sure
Address: Rua Almirante Cochrane, 162, Tijuca, Rio de Janeiro/RJ.


If you're planning to enjoy the best Forró parties in the South Zone of Rio de Janeiro, make sure to prepare to shine on the dance floor! Before venturing into the engaging movements of Forró, how about taking some classes to learn a few steps? After all, nobody is born knowing how to dance, right?

Take the opportunity to sign up for our FORRÓ CLASSES and become familiar with the rhythms and characteristic movements of this dance style. So, when the time comes to enjoy the parties in Rio de Janeiro, you'll be ready to shine!

Luiz Bragança

Luiz Bragança: Forró teacher, avid anime and cinema enthusiast.

Luiz Bragança has had a solid and long career in dance, which began when he was a teenager. Over the years, he has traveled and lived in various parts of the world, teaching Forró, samba de Gafieira, and other rhythms. His seasons included stints in Egypt, Switzerland, Italy, and various regions of Europe.

Additionally, Luiz Bragança speaks English and French fluently, and he loves teaching tourists due to the cultural and linguistic diversity.

Currently, Luiz teaches group and private classes at the Jaime Arôxa Copacabana Dance School.
